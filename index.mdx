OpenBeken (OpenBK7231T) is an open-source firmware that replaces the stock Tuya firmware on dozens of popular IoT chipsets. It brings MQTT connectivity, Home Assistant integration, an advanced scripting system, and a Tasmota-compatible command interface — all without vendor lock-in.

## Get started in four steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and flash the firmware">
    Grab the latest binary for your chip from the [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/openshwprojects/OpenBK7231T_App/releases) page and flash it using the GUI flash tool or UART/SPI method.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect to your WiFi network">
    After flashing, an access point named after your device appears. Connect to it, open `192.168.4.1`, go to **Config → WiFi**, enter your credentials, and reboot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure GPIO pins">
    Open the web interface, go to **Config → Pins**, and assign roles to each GPIO (relay, button, LED, ADC, etc.). You can apply a community device template to skip manual setup.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up MQTT and Home Assistant">
    Enter your MQTT broker details under **Config → MQTT**, then click **Start Home Assistant Discovery** to auto-register your device entities.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What OpenBeken supports

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="30+ Chipsets" icon="cpu">
    BK7231T/N, BL602, ESP32/ESP8266, RTL8710B/C/D, W600/W800, LN882H, ECR6600, and more.
  </Card>
  <Card title="MQTT + Home Assistant" icon="broadcast-tower">
    Full MQTT support with auto-discovery for lights, relays, and sensors.
  </Card>
  <Card title="TuyaMCU protocol" icon="plug">
    Communicate with the original Tuya MCU on dimmers, fans, and battery sensors.
  </Card>
  <Card title="LED control" icon="lightbulb">
    RGBCW LEDs via PWM or I2C drivers: SM2135, BP5758D, WS2812B, and more.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Power metering" icon="bolt">
    BL0942, BL0937, CSE7766, and other energy-metering chips with calibration.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Advanced scripting" icon="terminal">
    Event handlers, repeating timers, clock events, and LittleFS-based scripts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Supported platforms

OpenBeken runs on hardware from Beken, Bouffalo Lab, Espressif, WinnerMicro, Realtek, Lightning Semi, ESWIN, and more. See the full [Supported Platforms](/supported-platforms) page for a feature matrix.

<Note>
  OpenBeken maintains Tasmota command compatibility, so existing Tasmota rules, MQTT flows, and ioBroker integrations generally work without modification.
</Note>
